I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics, in the Arithmetic Geometry and Representation Theory group. Previously, I was a student of David Nadler at the University of California at Berkeley, and a Bing postdoctoral fellow at the University of Texas at Austin. I am interested in higher category theory and derived algebraic and symplectic geometry. Here is my CV.

Research

  • Tannaka duality and 1-affineness
    • Establishes an affineness result in the theory of sheaves of categories and applies it to Tannaka duality and to the study of relative tensor products and higher traces of ∞-categories of quasicoherent sheaves.

  • Derived ∞-categories as exact completions
    • Develops the theory of exact completions of regular ∞-categories and shows that in the additive context it recovers the theory of connective derived ∞-categories.

  • Classification of fully dualizable linear categories
    • Provides a description of the fully dualizable objects in the symmetric monoidal 2-category of cocomplete categories linear over a G-ring.

  • Derived Mellin Transform (With Tom Gannon)
    • This is an appendix to "Classification of nondegenerate G-categories" by Tom Gannon. Establishes an ∞-categorical version of the Mellin transform for D-modules on a torus.

  • Higher Quasicoherent Sheaves
    • My PhD thesis. The first half contains a number of results in the theory of enriched higher categories, culminating in a discussion of monads and the enriched monadicity theorem. The second half builds upon the two papers below and develops the theory of quasicoherent sheaves of (∞, n)-categories in derived algebraic geometry.

  • Higher sheaf theory I: Correspondences
    • Studies the problem of constructing representations of a higher category of correspondences. This is the first part of a series of papers that develop the theory of sheaves of higher categories in derived algebraic geometry.

  • Presentable (∞, n)-categories
    • Introduces a notion of presentable higher categories, and establishes some basic technical properties that will be used in the higher sheaf theory sequence.

Other writing

  • Cohomological methods in differential equations
    • My undergraduate thesis. Studies the theory of obstructions to the formal integrability of partial differential equations and its application to the equivalence problem for G-structures.
